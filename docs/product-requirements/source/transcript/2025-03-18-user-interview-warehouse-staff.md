# User Interview — Warehouse Staff — 18 Mar 2025

**Interviewee:** Lek (Warehouse Floor Staff)
**Interviewer:** Lisa (Business Analyst)
**Duration:** 30 min

---

## Context

One-on-one interview with a warehouse floor staff member to understand day-to-day pain points with current stock tracking.

---

## Transcript

**Lisa:** Can you walk me through a typical day of stock updates?

**Lek:** I come in at 8, check what deliveries arrived overnight. I count the pallets, then go to the whiteboard and update the numbers. If someone else already started, I wait or just write next to their numbers.

**Lisa:** What happens when there's a disagreement about the count?

**Lek:** That's the worst part. Last month a customer order was short by 12 units. The supervisor checked the Excel and the last entry was under my name — but I didn't make that entry. Someone else used the PC while I was on break. There's no login, no password, nothing. Everyone is "the same user."

**Lisa:** How did that get resolved?

**Lek:** It didn't really. The supervisor just told everyone to be more careful. But nothing changed.

**Lisa:** If you had a new system, what would matter most to you?

**Lek:** Knowing that my name is only on changes I actually made. And being able to update from my phone instead of walking to the PC every time.

**Lisa:** How comfortable are you with phone apps?

**Lek:** I use LINE and Facebook every day. If the app is as simple as those, no problem.

---

## Key Takeaways

- No user identity on current system — blame is assigned unfairly
- Strong desire for accountability and personal audit trail
- Mobile familiarity is high; app must be simple (LINE/Facebook level UX)
- Walking to a shared PC is a friction point throughout the day
