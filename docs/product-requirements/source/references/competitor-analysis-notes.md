# Competitor Analysis Notes

**Prepared by:** Jane (Product Owner)
**Date:** 15 Mar 2025

---

## Purpose

Quick survey of existing inventory management tools available to small warehouse businesses. Used to identify gaps and differentiation opportunities.

## Tools Reviewed

### 1. StockSimple (SaaS)

- **Target:** Small retail shops
- **Strengths:** Easy setup, barcode scanning, cloud-based
- **Weaknesses:** No real-time multi-user editing; batch sync only. No role-based access — all users have full edit rights
- **Pricing:** $29/month for up to 3 users

### 2. WarehouseKit (SaaS)

- **Target:** Mid-size warehouses (50+ SKUs)
- **Strengths:** Detailed reporting, forecasting, integrations with shipping providers
- **Weaknesses:** Complex setup; requires training. Overkill for a 3–6 person team. Expensive ($150+/month)
- **Pricing:** $150/month base + per-user fees

### 3. Shared Google Sheets

- **Target:** Anyone (DIY approach)
- **Strengths:** Free, familiar, multi-user editing
- **Weaknesses:** No role-based access, no audit trail per cell edit, no mobile-optimised input, easy to accidentally break formulas
- **Pricing:** Free

## Key Insights

| Insight | Implication for Our Product |
|---------|---------------------------|
| No tool offers real-time concurrent editing + role-based access at the small-team price point | This is our primary differentiation |
| Simple apps lack audit trails; complex apps are too expensive | We need audit + simplicity at a low price |
| All competitors assume desktop or tablet as primary device | Mobile-first is an underserved niche for warehouse staff |
